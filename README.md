Title: Strategic HR Analysis: Employee Attrition & Retention Report

Executive Summary: Analyzes the current 16.4% attrition rate and identifies a potential net saving of $3.96M through targeted intervention.

Business Problem: Addresses the financial and operational drain caused by 241 employee departures, focusing on recruitment and replacement costs.

Methodology: Utilized Python (Pandas/Seaborn) for Exploratory Data Analysis, T-tests for statistical validation of income factors, and turnover modeling for ROI projection.

Skills Demonstrated: Data simulation, Hypothesis Testing, Financial Modeling, and Data Visualization.

Results: Highlights that Sales (29.2%) and Overtime workers (25.3%) are the highest risk groups. Interestingly, income was not a statistically significant factor in leaving, suggesting cultural/workload issues are more prevalent than pay issues.

Next Steps: Recommends a $2,500/employee intervention program and an audit of overtime practices in the Sales department.
